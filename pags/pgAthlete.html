Jane Karla Rodrigues Gögel


Jane Karla Rodrigues Gögel, born in Aparecida de Goiânia on July 6, 1975, is a Brazilian Paralympic athlete who suffered from polio as a child and overcame breast cancer as an adult.
Jane has competed in five Paralympic Games: 2008 in Beijing, 2012 in London, 2016 in Rio de Janeiro, 2020 in Tokyo and 2024 in Paris.

Career:



Jane began her career in 2003, at the age of 28, when she was invited to try out different sports at an association for the physically disabled in the city of Goiânia where she lived. Jane loved table tennis and started playing this sport.

When she got better, she started winning national tournaments and participating in international tournaments.

At the 2005 Parapan Championship, she won her first medals and qualified for the 2006 World Championship. In the table tennis category, she participated in the Parapan American Games in Rio de Janeiro and the 2008 Beijing Paralympic Games.

In 2009, after having qualified for the 2010 World Championship in Korea, she was diagnosed with breast cancer.

Throughout her chemotherapy treatment, Jane continued to train, even winning a gold medal in the individual category and a silver medal in a group event in a tournament in Rio de Janeiro. In 2012, she was already participating in the Paralympic Games in London. In order to continue representing the Brazilian table tennis team, Jane would have to move to another city.



However, since she did not want to make that change, she had to give up the table tennis, but she did not give up the sport.

In 2015, he began training in Paralympic archery and that same year he qualified for the Parapan American Games in Toronto. Jane has lived in Portugal since 2018 where he trains in archery.
